Technology Team

Roles & Responsibilities

  • Software Engineer Builds apps and systems with code
  • AI Engineer Builds AI models and AI features
  • Frontend Developer Designs what users see and click
  • Backend Developer Builds servers, databases, APIs
  • Data Analyst Turns data into decisions
  • QA Engineer Finds bugs before customers do
  • DevOps Engineer Keeps systems running and deploying
  • Product Manager Decides what to build and why

Marketing Team

  • Social Media Manager Plans posts and grows community
  • Content Writer Writes blogs, captions, scripts
  • Graphic Designer Creates visuals, posters, brand designs
  • Video Editor Edits Reels, ads, and videos

Sales & Business

Roles & Responsibilities

  • Sales Executive Talks to customers and closes deals
  • Business Development Finds new partners and opportunities
  • Customer Success Helps customers succeed after buying
  • Account Manager Maintains long-term client relationships
Example: A Sales Executive at an ed-tech company helps a parent choose the right course. A Customer Success person makes sure the student keeps learning and renews.
Bharat AI Academy

Operations Team

Roles & Responsibilities

  • Operations Executive Makes daily work run smoothly
  • Project Coordinator Tracks timelines, tasks, and delivery
  • Admin Handles office systems and support
  • Logistics Moves products, materials, or resources
If Technology builds the product and Marketing attracts customers, Operations makes sure everything actually happens on time.

Sample LinkedIn Post · 1/6

What is AI?

AI is not magic. It is a tool that learns patterns from data and helps us make decisions faster.

In class we learned that AI can write, design, analyse, and answer questions — but it still needs human judgment.

Example: When you ask ChatGPT to rewrite a resume, you still decide what is true and what to send.

What is one task in your daily life where AI could already help you?

Sample LinkedIn Post · 2/6

Explain AI to a 10-year-old

If I had to explain AI to a 10-year-old, I would say: AI is like a very fast learner that studies many examples.

It does not think like humans. It predicts useful answers based on patterns it has seen.

Example: After seeing thousands of photos of cats, it can guess which new photo is a cat.

How would YOU explain AI to a younger sibling?

How AI will change India

AI will not only change big tech companies. It will change coaching centres, hospitals, farms, and small shops in India.

Students who learn AI early can help local businesses grow smarter — not just chase jobs in big cities.

Example: A medical store can use AI to write WhatsApp offers, track inventory, and answer common customer questions.
