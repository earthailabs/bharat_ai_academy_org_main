Meet Your Five AI Tools

A carpenter has many tools — hammer, saw, drill. An AI-powered person also has a toolkit. These five tools will be your daily companions.

💬

ChatGPT (OpenAI)

The most popular AI chat. Great all-rounder: writing, ideas, explanations, learning. Free plan is enough to start. chatgpt.com

✨

Gemini (Google)

Google's AI. Excellent with images, connects with Gmail/Docs, generous free plan. gemini.google.com

📝

Claude (Anthropic)

Loved for natural writing and careful, detailed answers. Great for long documents. claude.ai

🔍

Perplexity

AI + live web search. Answers with sources and links — best for research and current events. perplexity.ai

🛠️

Cursor

An AI editor installed on your laptop. Reads your folders and files, builds websites and projects with you. The heart of your AI Workspace. cursor.com

💡

Which is "best"?

None! Each shines at different tasks. Chapter 5 gives you the "which tool when" map.

📝 Remember This

The first four run in your browser (cloud AI). Cursor is installed on your laptop and can work with your local files. That difference is exactly what you learned in Part 1!

Creating Your Accounts — Step by Step

One email address unlocks everything. If you don't have a Gmail account, ask your instructor for help first.

ChatGPT

  • Open your browser and go to chatgpt.com
  • Click Sign up.
  • Choose Continue with Google and select your Gmail account.
  • Done! You'll see the chat box. Type "Hello!" to test it.

Gemini

  • Go to gemini.google.com
  • Sign in with the same Google account.
  • Accept the terms — the chat opens directly.

Claude

  • Go to claude.ai
  • Click Sign up, use Continue with Google.
  • Verify with the code sent to your email if asked.

Perplexity

  • Go to perplexity.ai
  • You can even use it without an account — but sign up with Google to save your history.
⚠️ Common Mistake

Creating different accounts with different emails and forgetting which is which. Use ONE Google account for all AI tools. Write your email in the front of this book if it helps!

📌 Note — Cursor

Cursor is installed as an app on the laptop (not just a website). We install it together in class: download from cursor.com → run installer → sign in with Google → File → Open Folder.

Tour of the Chat Interface

All AI chat tools look similar. Learn one, and you know them all.

The five parts of every AI chat screen

PartWhereWhat it does
Message boxBottom centreType your prompt here. Press Enter to send.
New chat buttonTop leftStarts a fresh conversation (AI forgets the old one!)
Chat historyLeft sidebarAll your old conversations — click to continue any
Attach / plus buttonNear message boxUpload files or photos for AI to read
Model pickerTopChoose which AI brain to use (fast vs smart)

Small but useful buttons

  • Copy — copies the AI's answer so you can paste it elsewhere.
  • Regenerate / retry — asks the AI to answer again differently.
  • Thumbs up/down — feedback that helps improve the AI.
  • Microphone — speak your prompt instead of typing (great for practice!).
🎯 Try It Now

In ChatGPT: (1) start a new chat, (2) ask "Give me 3 tips to study better", (3) press the copy button on the answer, (4) paste it into Notepad and save as study-tips.txt in your Practice Folder. You just connected AI with your file skills!

Free vs Paid — what do you actually need?

All five tools have free plans, and free is enough for this whole course. Paid plans give faster/smarter models and higher limits. Advice: stay free until you feel limited — then decide.

Conversations & Context — The One-Chat Rule

The single most useful habit for getting great AI answers.

AI remembers — but only inside one chat

Tell the AI in message 1: "I am a vegetarian college student in Rohtak." In message 10, ask: "Suggest a dinner plan." It will remember and suggest vegetarian food. That's context at work.

But if you start a new chat and ask the same question — the AI has no idea who you are. Context is gone.

💡 Analogy — The Shopkeeper

Visit the same shopkeeper daily, and he remembers you: "your usual dal and rice?" A new shop, and you must explain everything from scratch. One chat = your regular shop. New chat = new shop.

📝 Remember This — The One-Chat Rule

For one topic or project, stay in one chat. Start new chats for genuinely new topics. Give your chats clear names so you can find them again.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Opening a new chat for every message, then wondering why the AI "forgot everything". Also note: this chat memory is not permanent memory — each chat only remembers itself.

🎯 Try It Now — Feel the Context

Chat A: say "My name is [your name] and I love cricket." Then ask "What sport should I watch this weekend?" — notice it uses your interest.
Chat B (new chat): ask the same question directly — notice the generic answer. That difference IS context.

Choosing the Right Tool for the Task

You don't cut vegetables with a spoon. Match the tool to the job.

Your taskBest first choiceWhy
Write an email, essay, or captionChatGPT / ClaudeStrong, natural writing
Research with sources ("is this true?")PerplexitySearches the live web, shows links
Recent news or current eventsPerplexity / GeminiConnected to fresh information
Study help, explanations, practice questionsChatGPT / GeminiPatient, step-by-step teaching
Understand or summarise a long PDFClaude / GeminiHandle long documents well
Build a website or work with files/foldersCursorReads your project, edits real files
Ideas from a photo (e.g. "improve this poster")Gemini / ChatGPTBoth can see and discuss images
📝 Remember This

Write → ChatGPT/Claude · Research → Perplexity · Build → Cursor · Google-world tasks → Gemini.

🎯 Try It Now — Same Question, Three Tools

Ask all three — ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity: "What are the top 3 career skills for 2026? Keep it short." Compare the answers. Notice Perplexity shows sources. Which answer did you like most, and why?

Markdown — The Language AI Loves

Remember .md files from Part 1? Time to learn what's inside them. It takes 10 minutes and you'll use it forever.

🔑 AI Term — Markdown

Markdown is a way of writing plain text with simple symbols to show formatting: # for headings, - for lists, **bold** for bold. AI tools read and write Markdown naturally — in fact, ChatGPT's answers are formatted in Markdown!

The only 6 rules you need

You typeIt means
# My TitleA big heading
## Smaller TitleA sub-heading
- itemA bullet point
1. itemA numbered point
**important**Bold text
*note*Italic text

A complete example

My-Notes.md# My Study Plan ## Morning - Revise Part 1 notes - **Practice** file shortcuts ## Evening 1. Ask AI 5 questions 2. Write one prompt 3. Sleep by 11 pm
📝 Remember This

Why AI loves Markdown: it is plain text (no app lock-in), it is structured (AI understands headings and lists), and it is simple (humans can read it too). Your AI Skills files will all be .md files.

🎯 Try It Now

Open Notepad, write your weekly plan in Markdown using all 6 rules, and save it as my-plan.md in your Practice Folder. Then in ChatGPT, paste it and ask: "Improve my plan." Watch how well AI understands your structure!

Staying Safe with AI

AI tools are powerful and mostly safe — if you follow a few simple rules. These rules protect your money, identity, and reputation.

Rule 1 — Never share these with any AI

  • Passwords or OTPs
  • Bank details, card numbers, UPI PINs
  • Aadhaar / PAN numbers
  • Private photos or other people's personal information
💡 Analogy — The Public Diary

Treat an AI chat like writing on a postcard, not in a locked diary. Assume the company can see it. Would you write your bank PIN on a postcard? Never. Same rule here.

Rule 2 — Verify before you trust

AI can hallucinate (Part 2!). Before using an AI answer for anything important — health, money, exams, legal matters — check it against a reliable source or ask a real expert.

Rule 3 — AI for learning, not cheating

Use AI to understand topics, practise questions, and improve your own work. Copying AI answers into exams or assignments without understanding cheats only one person: you.

Rule 4 — Be careful with "too good" offers

Scammers use AI too — fake voices, fake photos, fake job offers. If a message seems urgent and demands money or OTPs, stop and verify by calling the person directly.

📝 Remember This — The Safety Formula

Share nothing private · Verify important facts · Learn, don't copy · Question urgent requests.
