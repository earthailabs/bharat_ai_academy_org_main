Section 1

Why Video Wins Attention

On Instagram, YouTube, and WhatsApp Status, moving pictures beat still pictures. Short video is the fastest way for a beginner to reach strangers.

Formats you will care about

FormatLengthWhere it lives
Reel / Short15–60 seconds (sometimes up to 90)Instagram, YouTube Shorts, Facebook
Long YouTube video8–20+ minutesYouTube
Talking-head tip30–90 secondsReels / Shorts / LinkedIn
Voice-over slideshow30–60 secondsReels (easy for beginners)
💡 Analogy

A still post is like a poster on a wall. A Reel is like a 45-second street performance. People stop because something is happening. AI helps you write the performance, speak it, add music, and cut it cleanly — even if you are camera-shy at first.

Two beginner paths (pick one for Project)

🎤

Path A — Face on camera

You speak to the phone. Highest trust. Best for personal brand.

🎧

Path B — Voice + visuals

AI voice or your recorded voice over B-roll / slides. Great if you are shy.

📝 Remember This

Hook in 3 seconds or people swipe away. Your first line and first visual must create curiosity, emotion, or a clear promise.

🔑 AI Term — B-roll

B-roll means supporting visuals — hands typing, street shots, product close-ups, screen recordings — shown while someone speaks. It makes talking videos feel richer.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Recording 5 minutes of random speaking with no script. Editing becomes painful and the message drifts. A 80-word script for a 40-second Reel saves an hour of cleaning up.

Scripts with AI

A script is the written plan of your video. Short videos need short scripts. Speak like a human, not like an essay.

Reel script structure (30–45 seconds)

SecondsPartJob
0–3HookStop the scroll ("Stop making this Instagram mistake…")
3–30Value1–3 clear tips or one story with a lesson
Last 5–10CTAFollow, save, comment, or share
📝 Remember This — Hook Formula

Problem + Curiosity or Number + Promise
Examples: "Most beginners ruin their Reels in the first second."
"3 Canva tricks that make posts look expensive."

Prompt — 40-second Reel scriptWrite a spoken script for a 40-second Instagram Reel. Audience: 12th-pass beginners in India learning AI. Topic: [your topic]. Structure: Hook (first line must be punchy) → 3 short tips → CTA. Tone: friendly, simple English, like talking to a younger cousin. Add [PAUSE] markers. Max 95 words. Also give 5 alternative hooks.
Prompt — YouTube long-form outlineCreate a YouTube video outline (10 minutes) on [topic] for beginners. Include: title options (5), hook for first 20 seconds, section timestamps, what B-roll to show in each section, and end screen CTA. Keep language simple.
🎯 Try It Now

Generate one Reel script on "What is an algorithm?" using the prompt above. Read it aloud. Cut any line that feels robotic. Keep the best hook.

AI Voices & Music

If you are shy on camera, start with voice-over. If you speak on camera, still use light music. Sound quality matters as much as video quality.

🔑 AI Term — Text-to-speech (TTS)

Text-to-speech means AI reads your script aloud in a chosen voice. Tools turn text into an audio file (MP3) that you drop into CapCut.

Voice tools beginners use

ToolWhat it doesNotes
ElevenLabsVery natural AI voicesFree monthly credits
CapCut TTSVoice inside the editorFast for Reels
Speechify / Google TTSQuick voice testsQuality varies
Your phone voice memoYour real voiceOften more trustworthy

Music — safe practice

  • Prefer music from CapCut library, Instagram trending audio (for IG), or royalty-free libraries.
  • Keep music quieter than speech — viewers must hear every word.
  • For YouTube monetisation later, be careful with copyrighted songs.
  1. Finalise your short script (under 100 words for a Reel)
  2. Paste into CapCut TTS or ElevenLabs → download audio
  3. Listen once: fix weird pronunciations by rewriting the word
  4. Import audio into CapCut timeline as the guide for visuals
⚠️ Common Mistake

Music louder than voice. Viewers leave. Rule of thumb: if you must choose, kill the music before you kill the clarity of speech.

AI Video Generators

AI can now create short video clips from text or images. Use them as B-roll spice — not as your entire personality on day one.

Tool (examples)Best beginner use
CapCut / TikTok AI effectsQuick stylised clips inside editor
Runway / Pika / Kling / LumaText-to-video or image-to-video clips
Google Veo (when available to you)Higher quality clips
InVideo / PictoryScript → auto video drafts
HeyGen / similarAI avatar presenters (use carefully)
💡 Analogy

AI video clips are like stock footage — useful seasoning. Your channel still needs a cook: your ideas, your script, your editing judgement. A full meal of only AI clips often feels empty and gets less trust.

Practical beginner approach

  1. Record yourself OR create AI voice for the script
  2. Generate 2–4 short AI clips that match key lines (3–5 seconds each)
  3. Mix with simple phone B-roll (desk, notebook, screen, street)
  4. Edit in CapCut so speech leads and clips support
Prompt — short clip description for a video AIA 4-second cinematic clip: top-down view of hands writing in a notebook beside a laptop on a wooden desk, soft morning light, shallow depth of field, realistic, no text on screen, steady camera
📝 Remember This

Audio first, visuals second. People forgive average visuals if audio is clear and the idea is strong. Rarely the opposite.

Editing with CapCut

CapCut is a free editor for phone and computer. It is enough for professional-looking Reels and Shorts.

Install & set up

  1. Install CapCut from Play Store / App Store, or desktop from the official site
  2. Create / sign in (can use TikTok or email)
  3. New project → set ratio 9:16 for Reels/Shorts

Edit a voice-over Reel — step by step

  1. Import your voice audio (or record voice inside CapCut)
  2. Import videos/photos/AI clips that match the script
  3. Place audio on the timeline first — this is your clock
  4. Cut visuals to change every 2–4 seconds (keeps energy)
  5. Tap Captions / Auto captions → edit mistakes
  6. Add light music under voice (lower volume)
  7. Export 1080p → save to camera roll / computer

CapCut skills worth learning first

SkillWhy it matters
Split / deleteRemove long pauses and mistakes
Auto captionsMost people watch without sound at first
SpeedTighten slow parts; avoid sounding rushed
Keyframes (basic)Gentle zoom on photos for life
TemplatesFast styled captions for beginners
🎯 Try It Now

Open CapCut. Import any 20-second clip from your phone. Add auto captions. Export once so you know the full path works on your device.

Captions, Covers & Quality Checks

Small finishing details separate "practice video" from "this looks ready to post".

Caption style tips

  • Large enough to read on a phone with one eye closed (almost)
  • High contrast — white text with dark outline/shadow works often
  • Highlight 1–2 key words in your brand accent colour
  • Do not cover faces — place captions lower or mid-safe zone
  • Fix every auto-caption error (names, Hindi words, AI terms)

Cover / thumbnail for the Reel

On Instagram, your Reel also needs a cover frame. Design a simple cover in Canva (1080×1920) with 3–5 words, or pick the clearest frame in CapCut/Instagram.

Pre-publish quality checklist

  • Hook is clear in the first 3 seconds
  • Voice is louder than music
  • Captions are accurate
  • No long silence or dead stares
  • Video ends with a CTA
  • Watched once on mute (captions carry it?) and once with sound
⚠️ Common Mistake

Exporting vertical video as 16:9 "by accident". Always check 9:16 before you start the CapCut project for Reels/Shorts.

Section 7

Your First Reel Project

This is the Module 2 climax. You will ship one complete short video using everything from Parts 1–4.

🎯 Try It Now — Part 4 Capstone

Make one 30–45 second Reel on any Module 1 or Module 2 lesson (example: "Local vs Cloud" or "What is an algorithm?").

Follow this exact checklist

  1. Idea: write one sentence: "After watching, a beginner will understand ___."
  2. Script: generate with AI → edit to your voice → under 100 words
  3. Audio: record yourself OR AI voice → clear MP3
  4. Visuals: either face recording OR 8–12 slides/clips that match lines
  5. Graphics: Canva title card (optional) + Reel cover
  6. Edit in CapCut: cuts, captions, music low, export 1080p 9:16
  7. Caption: 3–5 lines + CTA + 3–5 hashtags (from Part 1 learning)
  8. Publish to Instagram Reels or YouTube Shorts (or both)

Grading yourself (honest scorecard)

CheckYes / No
Would I stop scrolling for my own hook?
Can a beginner understand the tip without prior knowledge?
Is audio clear on phone speakers?
Are captions correct?
Did I include a CTA?
📝 Remember This

Done > perfect. Your first Reel teaches you more than watching 20 tutorials. Publish, note what felt weak, improve the next one.

Publishing & Growth Habits

One video rarely "goes viral" randomly. Creators grow with a simple weekly habit and learning from each post.

After you post — read the numbers

MetricWhat it hints
ViewsDid the system show it to people?
Average watch time / retentionWas the hook and pacing good?
Likes / saves / sharesWas the value worth keeping?
CommentsDid you invite a clear response?
FollowsDid the profile promise more of this?
💡 Analogy

Posting without checking insights is like cooking every day without tasting the food. The algorithm is a picky customer — your insights tell you what to change next time.

Simple weekly rhythm (beginner)

  • 3 Reels/Shorts per week OR 2 Reels + 1 carousel
  • Reuse one idea across formats: Reel + carousel + LinkedIn text
  • Sunday: fill next week's content calendar (from Part 1)
  • Keep a "hooks that worked" note on your phone
