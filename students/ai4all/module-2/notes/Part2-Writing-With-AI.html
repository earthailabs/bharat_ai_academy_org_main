Research with AI — Getting Facts Fast

Good content is built on good facts. These tools compress a day of research into 20 minutes.

Your research tools

ToolBest forFree?
Perplexity (perplexity.ai)Questions with sources — every answer shows links to where the facts came fromFree (Pro paid)
ChatGPT with SearchResearch + writing in one chatFree tier available
Gemini Deep ResearchLong, detailed research reports on one topicFree tier available
NotebookLM (notebooklm.google.com)Upload YOUR PDFs/notes — it answers only from them; can even make an audio discussionFree
Google Trends (trends.google.com)What people are searching, rising topics in IndiaFree
🔑 AI Term — Source

A source is the original place a fact comes from (a news site, research paper, official website). Trustworthy content always knows its sources. Perplexity is loved because it shows sources with every answer.

Step-by-step: research any topic in 20 minutes

  1. Define the question. Bad: "fitness". Good: "How much protein does a vegetarian Indian adult need daily?"
  2. Ask Perplexity the question. Read the answer AND click 2–3 sources to verify.
  3. Go deeper with follow-ups: "What are the common myths about this?", "What do recent studies say?", "Give India-specific data."
  4. Collect in a notes file: copy key facts + source links into a document (or a Markdown file, as you learned in Module 1).
  5. Ask AI to organise: paste your notes into ChatGPT — "Organise these notes into themes and highlight the 5 most surprising facts."
Prompt — research assistantI am researching: [topic] for a blog post aimed at beginners in India. Give me: (1) the 10 most important facts with sources, (2) 3 common myths people believe, (3) 2 recent developments, (4) 5 questions beginners ask about this topic. Keep language simple.
⚠️ Common Mistake

Trusting AI numbers blindly. AI can "hallucinate" — confidently state wrong facts (remember Module 1!). Rule: any number, date, or name that matters must be checked against a real source before you publish it.

Special tool: NotebookLM — AI that reads YOUR documents

Most AI tools answer from their general training. NotebookLM answers only from documents you upload — which means far fewer wrong facts. It is perfect for studying and for content research.

Step-by-step: use NotebookLM

  1. Go to notebooklm.google.com and sign in with your Google account.
  2. Click "New notebook" and give it a name (e.g., "Blog research — protein").
  3. Add sources: upload PDFs, paste website links, or paste text — up to dozens of sources in one notebook.
  4. Ask questions in the chat: "Summarise the main points", "What do these sources disagree on?", "Make a table of the key numbers."
  5. Try Audio Overview — NotebookLM creates a podcast-style discussion of your documents that you can listen to while travelling.
🎯 Try It Now

Upload this study pack's Part 1 (print to PDF first, or use any PDF you have) into NotebookLM. Ask: "Give me 5 quiz questions from this document." See how it answers only from the file — this is research superpower #1.

Finding content ideas people actually want

  • Google's "People also ask" — search your topic on Google; the questions box shows exactly what people want to know.
  • YouTube search suggestions — type your topic and see what auto-completes; those are real searches.
  • Comments sections — questions in comments of popular videos in your niche are free content ideas.
  • Google Trends — compare topics, see rising searches in India, filter by state.
Prompt — 25 content ideas from real questionsMy niche is [niche]. List 25 questions that beginners in India commonly search about this niche. Group them into: basics, how-to, mistakes, costs/money, and myths. For each group, mark the one question you think would make the most popular post.

Writing a Blog Post — Step by Step

Let's write a real 1000-word blog post using the 5-step workflow. Follow along with your own topic.

Step 1 — Research (you just learned this)

Collect 8–10 solid facts with sources on your topic using Perplexity, and save them in a notes file.

Step 2 — Outline

Prompt — blog outlineI want to write a blog post: "[working title]". Audience: complete beginners in India. Goal: [teach X / help them decide Y]. Here are my research notes: [paste notes]. Create an outline with: a hook intro idea, 5-7 H2 sections in logical order, 2-3 points under each, and a conclusion with a call-to-action. Do NOT write the full post yet.

Review the outline like an editor: remove weak sections, reorder, add anything missing from your own experience. This review is where your post becomes yours.

Step 3 — Draft, section by section

Prompt — write one section at a timeGreat outline. Now write the introduction and section 1 only. Rules: simple English (a 15-year-old should understand), short paragraphs of 2-3 lines, use "you" to talk to the reader, add one everyday Indian example, no heavy words like "utilize" or "furthermore". About 150 words per section.

Continue: "Now write section 2", and so on. Section-by-section gives better quality than asking for the whole post at once — and lets you correct direction early.

📝 Remember This

Outline first, draft second, one section at a time. Asking AI for a full article in one go produces generic text. Guiding it step by step produces YOUR article.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Accepting the first draft. The first draft is raw material, not the product. Professionals always run at least one improvement round (next page) before publishing.

Step 4 — Edit and improve

Prompt — self-critique roundAct as a strict editor. Review the full draft below and list: (1) any sentence that sounds robotic or repetitive, (2) claims that need a source or example, (3) places where a beginner would get confused, (4) the weakest section and how to fix it. Then rewrite ONLY the weak parts. [paste draft]
  • Read the post aloud once — your ear catches robotic lines that your eye misses.
  • Add one personal line or story only you could write. Readers connect with people, not text.
  • Verify every number and name against your research notes.

Step 5 — Title, SEO and publish

🔑 AI Term — SEO (Search Engine Optimisation)

SEO means writing so that Google can find and rank your post. The basics: use the words people search (keywords) in your title, first paragraph, and headings — naturally, not stuffed.

Prompt — titles + meta descriptionFor the blog post below, suggest: 5 SEO-friendly titles under 60 characters that include the keyword "[main keyword]", and a meta description under 155 characters that makes people want to click. [paste post or summary]

Captions & Hooks for Social Media

Short writing is harder than long writing. Every word must earn its place. Here's how to write captions that get read.

Anatomy of a great caption

Hook linestops the scroll
→
Valuethe useful middle
→
CTAwhat to do next
🔑 AI Term — CTA (Call To Action)

A CTA tells the reader what to do next: "Save this for later", "Comment YES if you agree", "Follow for part 2", "Share with a friend who needs this". Posts with a clear CTA get much more engagement.

Prompt — caption generatorWrite 3 caption options for my [Reel/post] about: [topic]. Structure each: hook line (under 8 words), 2-3 lines of value, one CTA. Tone: friendly, simple English, like talking to a friend. Add 3 relevant hashtags. Audience: [your audience].

Hook lines that work in text

TypeExample
Number promise"3 free AI tools that feel illegal to know"
Call out audience"If you're preparing for govt exams, read this."
Against common belief"You don't need a laptop to start editing."
Personal result"I wrote 4 blogs this week. Here's my exact method."
Warning"Delete this app if your phone storage is full."
📝 Remember This — Caption Formula

Hook (1 line) + Value (2–4 lines) + CTA (1 line) + 3–5 hashtags.
Short lines. One idea per line. White space is your friend.

Long Content — E-books, Guides & PDFs

A 20–50 page guide sounds scary. With the right method it's just a big blog post: same workflow, repeated chapter by chapter.

Step-by-step: create an e-book / guide

  1. Pick a promise: a good e-book solves ONE problem completely. Example: "The Complete Beginner's Guide to Freelancing in India."
  2. Build the chapter outline with AI: ask for 6–10 chapters, each with 3–5 sections — then edit the outline yourself.
  3. Research chapter by chapter (Perplexity/NotebookLM), keeping notes per chapter.
  4. Draft chapter by chapter — same section-by-section method as blogs. One chapter per day is a comfortable pace.
  5. Edit twice: once for facts, once for flow and simple language.
  6. Design & export: paste into Google Docs or Canva Docs → add headings, images, cover page → File → Download → PDF.
Prompt — e-book chapter outlineI'm creating a beginner-friendly guide: "[title/promise]". Audience: [audience]. Create a chapter plan: 8 chapters, each with a one-line goal and 4 section headings. Order them so a complete beginner can follow from zero to result. Mark which chapters need real data/research.
💡 Analogy

Writing an e-book is like cooking for a wedding. You don't cook one giant dish — you cook many normal dishes (chapters) one by one, with the same recipes (workflow) you already know. Scale comes from repetition, not magic.

Making the PDF look professional

  • Google Docs: use Heading 1/2 styles, insert a table of contents (Insert → Table of contents), add page numbers.
  • Canva Docs / Canva "Document" templates: beautiful ready designs — replace the text, export as PDF.
  • Add a cover page with the title, your name, and one strong image (you'll make one in Part 3).
  • Keep paragraphs short and add a "key takeaway" box at each chapter end — just like this book!
📌 Note

E-books make great free gifts to grow an audience ("Follow + comment GUIDE and I'll send the PDF") and are also sold by creators for ₹99–₹999. Your first one is for practice; the skill lasts a lifetime.

Market Research Reports with AI

Businesses pay well for organised information: "Who are my competitors? What do customers complain about? What's trending?" You can now produce this in hours, not weeks.

What goes inside a simple market research report

SectionAnswers the question
1. Market overviewHow big is this market? Is it growing?
2. Customer profileWho buys? Age, needs, budget, where they spend time online
3. CompetitorsWho else sells this? Their prices, strengths, weaknesses
4. TrendsWhat is rising and falling in this space?
5. OpportunitiesGaps nobody is filling — where a new player can win
6. Recommendations3–5 clear suggested actions

Step-by-step: research a local business market

  1. Define the exact question: "Should a new home-tiffin service start in Rohtak, and how should it stand out?"
  2. Use Gemini Deep Research or Perplexity: ask for market size, trends, and typical pricing for tiffin services in Indian tier-2 cities — with sources.
  3. Study real competitors: search Google Maps + Instagram for existing services. Note their prices, menus, reviews. Read the bad reviews — complaints = opportunities.
  4. Organise with AI: paste all your raw notes and ask AI to structure them into the 6 report sections above.
  5. Write recommendations yourself — this is the part clients value most, and where your judgement matters.
  6. Format in Google Docs → add a cover, charts if any → export PDF.
Prompt — structure a research reportBelow are my raw research notes about [market/business idea] in [city/region]. Organise them into a professional report with these sections: Market Overview, Customer Profile, Competitors (as a comparison table), Trends, Opportunities, Recommendations. Keep language simple and factual. Mark any place where data is missing so I can research more. [paste notes]

Pick any local business type in your city (salon, gym, tiffin, coaching). Produce a 3–5 page mini market report using the steps above. This is a real freelance-worthy skill.

Editing, Fact-Checking & the Human Touch

Readers can smell pure AI text. These habits make your writing trustworthy and human.

The 5-point pre-publish checklist

  • Facts: every number, name, and date verified against a source
  • Voice: at least one personal example or opinion only you could add
  • Simplicity: no word your younger cousin wouldn't understand
  • Robotic phrases removed: delete "In today's fast-paced world…", "delve into", "game-changer", "unlock the power of"
  • Read aloud test: if a line feels awkward to say, rewrite it
⚠️ Common Mistake — The copy-paste publisher

Copy AI output → paste → publish → looks like everyone else's content → nobody follows. The extra 20% human effort (facts, stories, voice) is exactly what separates content that grows from content that gets ignored.

Plagiarism and originality

🔑 AI Term — Plagiarism

Plagiarism means presenting someone else's work as your own. Copying another creator's article or script word-for-word is plagiarism — it can get your account penalised and destroys trust. Learning from many sources and writing in your own words is not.

  • Never copy a competitor's post text or script directly — take the idea, make your own version with your examples.
  • When you quote someone's data or words, name the source. It increases (not decreases) your credibility.
  • AI-generated text can accidentally echo common phrasing — one more reason to rewrite in your voice.
